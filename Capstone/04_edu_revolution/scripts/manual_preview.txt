1. Introduction

learning beyond the classroom . Through Revenue Generation opportunities, Projects &

Aligned with the University's vision, EDU Revolution empowers students to earn while they 
learn  and create opportunities to earn their own program fee  through innovation, 
entrepreneurship, and real -world achievements. Several academic initiatives have been

 3. Processing of Student Nominations  
EDU Revolution provides multiple pathways for students to convert their learning, skills, 
achievements, and real -world experiences into academic benefits. The following sections 
outline the eligibility conditions, acceptable activities, required proofs, an d benefits available 
under each category.  
Criteria Sheet : A Criteria Sheet  is a structured framework that clearly defines the eligibility 
conditions, required proofs, evaluation parameters, and corresponding academic benefits  
for various EDU Revolution categories. It ensures transparency, standardization, and 
consistency  in how student achievements are assessed and mapped to academic outcomes.  
Detailed description is provided in the subsequent section . 
Standing Committee: The Head of School (HOS) shall constitute a Standing Committee to 
review student nominations submitted under EDU Revolution and determine the benefits to be 
extended to eligible students.  
Composition:  
• The Standing Committee shall consist of 5 to 7 members.  
• The HOS may include additional members at their discretion, if required.  
• All relevant committee coordinators shall be members of the Standing Committee.  
Roles and Responsibilities:  
• The Standing Committee shall evaluate student nominations, verify supporting 
documents, and assess eligibility in accordance with the prescribed guidelines and 
criteria.  
• The Committee shall recommend the appropriate EDU Revolution benefits, including 
course equivalence, grade upgradation, attendance relaxation, duty leave, transcript 
recognition, or any other approved benefit.  
• The Committee shall ensure transparency, consistency, and fairness in decision -making 
and communicate its recommendations within the stipulated timelines.

4. Revenue Generation  
Definition:  Revenue Generation refers to income earned by students through entrepreneurial, professional, technical, creative, or service -based 
activities by applying their knowledge and skills. Aligned with the University's "Earn Your Fee"  vision, it promotes self -reliance, innovation, and 
real-world learning.  
Eligibility  
1. Revenue must be generated through university -approved activities and verified by the designated University Committee.  
2. There is no minimum CGPA requirement  for availing Academic benefits.  
3. Academic benefits shall be applicable only to courses in which the student has secured a passing grade.  
4. Students seeking attendance benefits must maintain a minimum of 60% attendance (excluding bonus attendance and Duty Leave) in the concerned 
semester.  
5. Revenue generated through family -owned businesses or family enterprises shall not be considered  
Important Notes  
1. Students registered under revenue generation must appear for the End Term Examination (ETE) irrespective of the benefit cl aimed.  
2. Course mapping shall be carried out only by the Head of the School. Courses involving third -party evaluation, dissertations, internships, projects, or 
similar components shall not be eligible for course mapping.  
3. Students applying for Course Equivalence must submit the prescribed Undertaking Form.  
4. Revenue proof and supporting documents must be submitted at least 15 days before the last teaching day of the semester.  
6. The decision of the designated University Committee regarding verification and approval of benefits shall be final and bin ding.  
Academic Benefits  
Based on verified revenue generation, students may avail CA Equivalence, Attendance Benefit, Grade Upgradation, and Course Eq uivalence, as as

5. NPTEL/ Proctored MOOCs/Certifications  
Definition:  NPTEL, Proctored MOOCs, and Certifications refer to structured learning 
programs offered by recognized platforms and institutions that enable students to acquire 
additional knowledge, skills, and competencies beyond the regular curriculum.  
 
Eligibility  
1. The course must be completed through a recognized NPTEL, Proctored MOOC, or 
Certification platform approved by the University.  
2. Students seeking Complete Course Equivalence must have a minimum CGPA of 7.0 at 
the time of application.  
3. There shall be no CGPA requirement for availing partial course equivalence benefits as 
per the approved policy.  
4. The course content and learning outcomes must be relevant to the mapped university 
course.  
5. Students must submit the prescribed undertaking form along with proof of registration 
and completion of the course.  
Important Notes:  
1. Students applying for Complete Course Equivalence must compulsorily submit the 
prescribed Undertaking Form along with the examination registration proof.  
2. Course mapping shall be carried out only by the Head of the School or the designated 
academic authority.  
3. Benefits shall be granted only after successful verification of course completion and 
supporting documents.  
4. The decision of the Standing Committee regarding course mapping and approval of 
benefits shall be final and binding.  
Academic Benefits:  
Based on successful completion and verification of NPTEL/Proctored

6. Projects  
Definition:  A Project is a structured, outcome -driven activity undertaken by a student to solve a real -world problem, develop innovation, or 
conduct academic, industry -oriented, or field -based research under guided supervision.  
Eligibility  
1. A minimum CGPA of 6.0 is the standard eligibility requirement for availing Project -based benefits.  
2. Duty Leave -related benefits  shall be granted only if the student maintains a minimum of 65% attendance , excluding bonus attendance 
and Duty Leave.  
3. If a project requires engagement during scheduled class hours, the student must obtain prior approval from the Standing Co mmittee to avail 
the additional benefit of Duty Leave.  
4. Academic benefits shall be granted only upon submission of complete documentation and successful verification by the Stand ing 
Committee.  
5. Benefits shall be applicable only in the mapped course(s) as recommended by the Standing Committee.  
6. All submitted nominations shall undergo verification and evaluation by the Standing Committee.  
Important Notes  
1. Academic benefits shall be granted only in the mapped course(s) associated with the approved project.  
2. Students may explore and nominate from the list of projects available on the EDU Revolution Portal.  
3. Students may also propose their own project(s) through the EDU Revolution interface, subject to evaluation and approval by  the Standing 
Committee.  
4. The Standing Committee may recommend one or more academic benefits based on the nature, scope, outcomes, and impact of the  project.  
5. The decision of the Standing Committee and approval of the Head of School regarding project mapping and academic benefits shall be final 
and binding.

7. Internship beyond Curriculum  
Definition: An internship is a supervised, work -based learning experience that provides practical skills and industry exposure. Internships undertaken 
beyond the curriculum, which demonstrate substantial academic relevance, professional value, and meaningful learning outcomes, shall be considered 
under this policy  
 
Eligibility:  
1. A minimum CGPA of 6 is the standard eligibility requirement for internship -based benefits. In exceptional cases where the internship 
demonstrates outstanding quality, students with a CGPA below 6 may also be considered  
2. If an internship requires engagement during scheduled class hours, the student must obtain prior approval from standing co mmittee to avail 
additional benefit of Duty Leave (10% or 15% DL of overall attendance).  
3. For any Duty leave related benefits student must maintain minimum 65% attendance  without any bonus/duty leave  
4. All submitted nominations will undergo a verification process by the Standing Committee.  
5. Students are advised to prefer internships during breaks, off -hours, or weekends.  
 
Important Note:  In cases where the total internship stipend exceeds ₹1,00,000, the student may be considered for a complete course waive r 
against course approved by the Standing Committee  
 
Academic Benefits:  Students can avail the academic benefits like Continuous Assessment, Grade up, Course exemption and duty leave  based
